TAG Security's working groups are where the real work happens. Each group focuses on a specific area of cloud native security, holding regular meetings and producing publications, whitepapers, and best practices.

All working groups are open to the public — everyone is welcome to join, listen, and contribute.

## Active Working Groups

| Working Group | Focus Area |
|---------------|------------|
| [Automated Governance](automated-governance/) | Integrating security, compliance, and auditability into CI/CD pipelines |
| [Catalog of Supply Chain Compromises](catalog-of-supply-chain-compromises/) | Tracking and analyzing real-world supply chain attacks |
| [Commons](commons/) | Cross-foundation collaboration with OpenSSF and Linux Foundation bodies |
| [Compliance](compliance/) | Bridging technical security with legal and regulatory requirements |
| [Security Assessments](security-assessments/) | Evaluating and improving the security posture of CNCF projects |
| [Software Supply Chain](supply-chain-security/) | Best practices and frameworks for securing the software supply chain |
| [Research](research/) | Publishing articles, blog posts, and thought leadership |

## How to Get Involved

1. **Pick a working group** that interests you
2. **Read its landing page** below and follow the GitHub link for details
3. **Join the meeting** — all meetings are public and listed on the [CNCF Community Calendar](https://www.cncf.io/calendar/)
4. **Introduce yourself** on the `#tag-security` channel on [CNCF Slack](https://slack.cncf.io/)
5. **Look for `good first issues`** on [GitHub](https://github.com/cncf/tag-security/issues)

## Meeting Schedule

Each working group sets its own meeting cadence. Check the individual landing pages and the [CNCF Calendar](https://www.cncf.io/calendar/) for the latest schedule.
